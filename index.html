Exoplanet Research April 28, 2025 — Published in Nature Astronomy

Biosignature Detection in TRAPPIST-1e Atmosphere

Spectroscopic analysis of the TRAPPIST-1e exoplanet atmosphere reveals water vapor signatures at 3.5σ confidence and methane detection at 2.8σ, consistent with potential biological or geological production. This represents the most compelling biosignature candidate detected to date outside the Solar System.

Lead InvestigatorDr. Elena Vasquez, Exoplanet Science Division
InstrumentHigh-Resolution Spectrograph (HRS-4)
Data ArchiveSTScI-GTO-2025-0847

About AstraNova

The United Interstellar Coalition's civilian space agency, dedicated to the peaceful exploration of space and the advancement of human knowledge.

Our Mission

The AstraNova Space Administration is responsible for the civilian space program of the United Interstellar Coalition. Founded in 2024, AstraNova carries forward the legacy of previous space programs while charting a new course for deep space exploration and the sustainable expansion of human presence beyond Earth.

Our mission is threefold: to expand human knowledge of the solar system and beyond, to develop the technologies and infrastructure required for long-duration space operations, and to foster international cooperation in the peaceful uses of outer space.

With research centers on three continents, a fleet of six active spacecraft, and partnerships with 42 space agencies worldwide, AstraNova operates at the frontier of human scientific endeavor.

Organizational Pillars

01

Discovery

Advancing human understanding of the cosmos through robotic and crewed exploration of the solar system and beyond. Our missions address fundamental questions about the origins and evolution of planetary systems.

02

Innovation

Developing breakthrough propulsion systems, life support technologies, and scientific instruments that enable access to previously unreachable destinations. Our technology development office funds over 200 active programs.

03

Collaboration

Uniting 42 space agencies and hundreds of research institutions in pursuit of shared scientific objectives. International cooperation is the foundation of all major AstraNova programs.

04

Open Science

Committed to making all mission data and research findings freely available to the global scientific community. Our data archive of 2.4 petabytes serves over 340 institutions worldwide.
